How many customer interviews have you actually done?
A real interview means talking to someone (face-to-face, call, or message) and asking them about their problem — not just "Would you buy this?". Good example: "Tell me about the last time you were frustrated with X." Even 1 conversation counts!
0 interviews Aim for 5 — but even 1 teaches you something! 🎉
📊 Research shows that 5 interviews reveal ~85% of the patterns you'll find. Fewer than 5 is like reading 1 weather forecast and deciding to move countries — interesting, but not enough data!

{% for opt in ['Friends and family only', 'Mix of known + strangers', 'Mostly strangers', 'Online community / survey'] %} {% endfor %}
💡 Friends are a great start! But try to talk to at least 1 person who isn't a friend — they give more honest feedback since they won't say yes just to be nice.
What type of questions did you mostly ask?
Leading questions push people toward the answer you want — e.g. "You'd buy this if it was cheap, right?" (they say yes to be nice, not because it's true). Open questions let them tell their real story — e.g. "Tell me about the last time you felt frustrated with X." Open questions give you honest data.
🚫 Leading = "You'd buy this, right?" (biased)  ·  ✅ Open-ended = "Tell me about the last time you needed X" (honest)
{% for opt in ['Leading questions', 'Mix of both', 'Open-ended questions'] %} {% endfor %}
⚠️ Leading questions give unreliable data. People say yes to be polite — not because they'd actually buy. Try asking open questions next time: "What's the hardest part about X for you?" or "Walk me through the last time this was a problem."
