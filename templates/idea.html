💭 Why does this chapter matter?
A clear, specific business idea is the foundation of everything. Without knowing exactly who you're selling to, what problem you solve, and where you'll sell — your mentor can't give useful feedback, and customers won't understand what you do. This chapter turns a vague dream into a focused pitch! 🎯
📚 What you'll learn in this chapter:
  • How to write a clear, specific business idea sentence
  • The WHO, PROBLEM, WHERE framework for pitching
  • Why a specific niche beats "selling to everyone"
  • How to score your idea's maturity level
⚠️ When businesses skip this chapter:
Google+ launched in 2011 as a social network for "everyone." With no specific niche or customer problem to solve, it failed to stand out against Facebook — despite billions in backing. This chapter exists so you build for someone, not everyone.
