How LrnBiz Works

Architecture overview — for teachers, evaluators, and curious students

The Triple Truth Scoring Pipeline

Every chapter in LrnBiz is scored three simultaneous ways. This is the core architectural claim: a single student submission passes through three independent scoring systems, each with a different view of the plan.

🐍
Layer 1 — Rules Check (Symbolic)
Deterministic, zero AI

37 hand-crafted business validation rules across 5 JSON files are evaluated against the student's answers using a symbolic rule engine. No machine learning — pure if/then logic. This layer runs offline, never hallucinates, and is fully auditable. Each rule carries a score_impact on one of six business axes: Passion, Energy, Gold, Influence, Knowledge, Target.

🤖
Layer 2 — AI Optimist (Pure LLM)
Blind encourager, temperature=0

A large language model reads only the student's raw answers — no rules context, no violation list. It is asked to self-report a score alongside its feedback. Temperature is fixed at 0.0 for full determinism. This layer intentionally over-praises: it is the "sycophantic AI" baseline that students learn to see through.

🧠
Layer 3 — Hybrid Mentor (Grounded LLM)
Honest coach, rules-aware

The same LLM receives the student's answers plus the full list of rule violations from Layer 1 as context. It is instructed to give honest, Socratic feedback. Its score becomes the Mentor Score — the primary grade. Because it is grounded in the symbolic rules, it cannot overlook structural problems the way the AI Optimist can.

Sycophancy Detection

The AI Honesty Gap is calculated as:

Sycophancy Gap = AI Optimist Score − Mentor Score

A large positive gap means the AI was over-generous — it praised a plan that had real structural problems. Students see this number after every chapter. The pedagogical goal is for students to learn to question AI praise and look for the grounded evaluation instead. Research shows most LLMs have a strong sycophancy bias at default settings; this system makes that bias visible in real time.

Each rule's score_impact targets one or more axes. As students fix rule violations, specific axes rise. The radar visualises which entrepreneurial dimensions are strong vs weak — a more actionable signal than a single aggregate score.

Evaluation Lab

The Evaluation Lab (/eval) contains 20 synthetic student personas with known profiles — covering a range of business types, grade levels, budgets, and common mistakes. Running triple-truth scoring on these personas benchmarks the scoring engine independently of real student data. This is the reproducibility layer: results on personas are deterministic and comparable across code changes.

Teacher Features

📋
Class Codes — Teachers create a class and share a 6-character code. Students enter it on the Scores page to register. Their snapshot (name, chapter progress, scores) appears in the class roster automatically.
🔗
Share Links — Students can generate a read-only share link to their completed plan, which any teacher or peer can view without logging in.
📊
Research Log — Every scoring event is written to an audit log accessible at /admin/research (requires HTTP Basic Auth). Exportable as JSON or CSV for further analysis.
